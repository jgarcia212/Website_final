This is my last semester at UT Austin, and I would say that the data science skills I have learned will be useful going forward. This fall, I will be attending the Long School of Medicine at UT Health San Antonio, and I will definitely be looking into becoming involved in research. Maybe I could woo a professor by offering to help with data analysis, who knows!

For most of the computational biology course, we focused on learning R for data analysis. However, towards the end, we began to learn about python. Gaining knowledge in both programs has its benefits, and it’s also cool how the two programs can interact.

Here is a nice example of how Python can be used in biological research from class. The objective was to find certain restriction enzyme sites in one large string of nucleotides. With just one line of code, this was easy to do.
